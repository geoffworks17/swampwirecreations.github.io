Sate Display Piece

Sunday, Sep 27, 2020
Blog

Slow Death Games is an indie game company that sells the Starbreach dice and rulebook, along with some awesome punk / youth subculture minis for their game “Wild in the Streets”. A few months ago on Instagram, they announced they were throwing some free stickers into new orders. I figured it was time to pull the trigger and order some minis. Yes, free stickers are what got me. In particular, the “Love Miniatures, Hate Fascism” piece.

When my order arrived, I was so excited to see they tossed in a bunch of stickers with my dice and Sate figure.

Base Coat

I started out with a gray primer and black wash, which really brought out the details I had missed when I first looked over the piece.

I then painted up the pants with a blue ink with some transparency to let the underpainting show though. To highlight the edges, I did a drybrush with white mixed with a bit of that blue ink.

At first, I was going to do a blue hoodie, but that did not look quite right, so I re-primed the upper part gray and decided to go a bit darker.

Details

After adding the main blocks of color, I started filling in details, like the buttons, shoe trim, and metal buckles on the messenger bag.

Looking over the pictures, I noticed a few spots I needed to clean up in the future, like in inconsistent covering on the back of the white spray paint can.

Backdrop

I had a pretty good idea of where I wanted to go with this display piece, but I needed to make a few bits to bring it together. I wanted him standing on some sort of platform, with a brick wall behind him with some fresh graffiti.

To start I used a piece of 28mm scale brick wall I found on thingiverse. I wanted it to be a bit more imposing, so I scaled it up from 30mm to about 50mm tall and called them cinderblocks instead of bricks. This was primed gray with my airbrush.

Using a vinyl cutter, I cut out an Anti-Fascist flag image into two separate black and red stencils. I figured the airbrush would make a nice “spraypaint” effect, and I was happy with the result.

Next, I finished up the Sate figure, repainting one of the paint cans black to match the tag behind him, and added the black to his shows. I also painted the whites of his eyes, but punked out on dotting the pupils. This display piece will live on my desk, and I can live with that blank stare. We can call it a “Blank Stare of Defiance.”

Finished

The bottom piece I modeled in Blender. To approximate a curb, I created a base layer, a raised platform, and beveled the edge. There is a recessed space on the back for the wall, and a small slot in the middle to fit the bar attached to his feet. After some sanding and painting, I am happy with how smooth this turned out. The curb is primed gray, with a sepia wash to give it that “sidewalk concrete” look.

I had a ton of fun putting this piece together. With miniature painting and tabletop wargaming, I figured I would enjoy getting better at speedpainting and churning out squads of 5, 10, or 15 units, but this was a pleasant (maybe even preferable!) change of pace. It was fun to work on this here and there and put a little more effort into this as an art piece and explore some of its underlying themes.

I wanted to keep with the “Love Minis, Hate Fascism” that initially brought this piece to my workbench. Painting this up gave me the opportunity to explore this symbol and the current political and social climate I find myself in. I do not advocate for violence against any group for exercising a right to free speech. It is important to remember that words can quickly become actions, and a tolerance for hate speech allows it to take root, and grow into actions of violence and oppression of others. Personally, I see Anti-Fascist Action as a counterpoint to the rise in Nationalism, Authoritarianism, and Far-Right Extremism that threaten the democracy of this country and the truth that we are all members of the human race and should treat each other as such.
